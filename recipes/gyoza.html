Gyoza

Delicious Homemade Gyoza

Gyoza, or Japanese dumplings, are a perfect balance of crispy and juicy, packed with savory filling and wrapped in a delicate, pan-fried dumpling skin. These dumplings are pan-fried to golden perfection, then steamed to create the ideal texture. Follow this easy step-by-step guide to making authentic gyoza at home!

Ingredients

For the Gyoza Filling:

  • 1/2 lb (225g) ground pork
  • 1 cup (100g) Napa cabbage, finely chopped
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper

For the Gyoza Wrappers:

  • 25-30 round gyoza wrappers

For Cooking:

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) water

For Dipping Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp sesame oil
  • Optional: chili oil or grated garlic for extra flavor

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

  • Finely chop the Napa cabbage and sprinkle with a little salt. Let it sit for 5 minutes, then squeeze out excess moisture.
  • In a large bowl, mix ground pork, cabbage, green onions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, sugar, and white pepper until well combined.

Step 2: Assemble the Gyoza

  • Place a small spoonful of filling in the center of each gyoza wrapper.
  • Lightly moisten the edges of the wrapper with water.
  • Fold the wrapper in half and pleat the edges to seal.

Step 3: Cook the Gyoza

  • Heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  • Place gyoza flat side down in the pan and cook until the bottoms are golden brown (about 2 minutes).
  • Add 1/4 cup (60ml) of water to the pan and cover immediately. Steam for about 5 minutes or until the water evaporates.
  • Uncover and cook for another minute to crisp up the bottoms.

Step 4: Prepare the Dipping Sauce

  • Mix soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil in a small bowl.
  • Optionally, add chili oil or grated garlic for extra heat and flavor.

Step 5: Serve & Enjoy!

  • Serve the gyoza hot with dipping sauce on the side.
  • Enjoy your homemade Japanese gyoza!
